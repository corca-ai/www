Is everyone eating? “Is everyone eating?”

2022-05-20Choong-Hwan Han (MLOps Engineer)

“Is everyone eating?”

How often do you hear this?

These are very kind and nice words, but strangely enough, I don't think it's easy to hear them often these days.

In Corca, Orcabot asks twice a day.

Are you all eating?

“ ?”

At 10:30 a.m., Orcabot is speaking kindly to me again today.

Corca has a #bob channel on Slack.

In response to the friendly questions Orcabot asks on this channel, what if you decide whether to eat or not, or express your desired menu using various emoticons?

We are organizing groups of members who will eat together today.

If you are curious about the development history of Orcabot, please also read Part 1 of Orcabot development history.

Whether at school or work, there is a saying called ‘food friend.’ I think anyone who has ever wondered, ‘Who should I eat with today?’ will know the importance of ‘food friend.’

At Corca, you don't have to worry about friends because Orcabot lets team members know who they will eat with so that they can eat with a variety of people.

What people want to eat every day may not be as good as MBTI, but it does seem to tell a lot about a person's tastes or mood that day.

In that sense, having the same taste is a very convenient and good thing.

🥗 There are members who always eat salad for lunch 🍣 If you especially want to eat sushi or spicy octopus today,

These are Corcans who are looking for members they want to work with on the #bob channel.

👀 Is there a reason why Corcas are so serious about food?

This comes to mind, there is a saying that people who know a lot want to eat a lot.

“ ” .

I think the Corca people are very curious and passionate people, so they are sincere about their food!

I think another reason is Corca’s generous food support and “ ”.

One of Corca's benefits is that generous lunch and dinner meals are provided during working hours.

In particular, at Corca, voluntary tech seminars are held in the evenings and some members also study, so there are quite a few members who work in the evenings.

Therefore, there are many cases where we eat dinner together and work together instead of the typical company working hours of 9 to 6, and since both lunch and dinner are covered, it is a work environment where team members can eat more comfortably and work hard.

I've seen large companies run in-house cafeterias to provide meals to their employees, or provide a certain amount of meal expenses in their salaries, but I was very surprised that they provide employees with a generous limit for one meal every time!

I think that’s why Corcans enjoy ‘meal time’ with a joyful and happy heart and burn with passion during work hours.

I think that mealtime has an important meaning, going beyond just eating something to satisfy hunger, it also allows you to build human bonds by sharing your concerns about work and life with the people you eat with.

A friend of rice is a real friend 😆 .

We are waiting for many people to become ‘Jjinchin’ who eat with Corcans.
