[Ceal Interview] The process of new employees adapting to work: gaining context through conversation and creating milestones by hand

_This is an article compiled from an interview with Ceal, an agent who works together in the company, about the experiments and trials and errors that emerged from the AX sharing meeting._

Q2. How were you working before that?

At first, I tended to keep the problem to myself for a long time and look at implementation as a goal in itself. It was important to use agents to learn and get help, but it was difficult to fully understand the context of the company or the direction of work.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > How were you doing it before, and what was inconvenient?

Jeongmin’s response

From the perspective of a new employee, the context of the existing company, the way it communicated, and the way it worked were not yet well understood. So there was a limit to finding direction just by thinking alone for a long time.

I felt that it was good to learn while actively using the agent. However, in the end, I organized my thoughts by thinking about them myself.How to write by handThis was more effective.

---

Q3. At what point did you decide you needed to do something differently?

The turning point was pair work and conversation. The thoughts I had been organizing on my own became concrete more quickly through conversations with people, and I began to see that blog improvement was not just about producing output but also included the flow of organizational introduction.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > At what point did you decide, “I need to design this differently”?

Jeongmin’s response

From talking with others, I learned that the goal is to bring about organizational change within the topic of blog improvement.

First I thought about implementation, then I specified the steps, and then I started thinking about the entire process of introducing the organization. As the scope of work gradually expands, rather than just thinking about it alone,How to communicate and communicate with other peopleI felt this was necessary.

---

Q4. So what actually changed?

The biggest change was the way we collaborated. When I had some thoughts or concerns arose, I started talking to other people rather than keeping them to myself for longer.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > So what did we actually change? What has changed: information flow, tools, skills, or collaboration?

Jeongmin’s response

First of allCollaboration styleIt seems to be.

Rather than thinking about it alone for a long time, I found that it is much more efficient and faster, both in terms of sharing the work and making progress, to discuss it with others once the thoughts have become more concrete and concerns arise.

I also felt that it was good to learn while actively using the agent in the process. But in the end, I decided to organize my thoughts by thinking about them myself.It is effective to write by handIt seems to be.

Q5. Was there a specific scene where that judgment was revealed?

The scene that Jeongmin mentioned was not simply “the conversation was good,” but the flow of organizing things in one’s own way after the conversation. Working in pairs made the work progress faster, and as I organized my thoughts by hand, milestones became clearer.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > Is there one moment you can show through a scene or screenshot that reveals that judgment?

Jeongmin’s response

I have had several experiences where working in pairs has made the work progress faster than working alone.

Also, after that process, I organized my thoughts by hand and the milestones of the work became concrete, and thanks to that, the work seemed to progress faster.

The important flow in this scene is as follows:

  • 🤝 pair workQuickly overcome the blocked point by yourself.
  • 💬 Do not pass on the context gained from the conversation.
  • ✍️ I reorganize my work by hand and change it into stages and milestones.

---

Q6. What was different from what you first thought while trying it?

The initial misunderstanding was that automation and implementation were the focus. But in reality, automation was less of a goal and more of a means to better the way work was shared and introduced within the organization.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > Did you think wrong at first, or was there an approach that didn't work out for you?

Jeongmin’s response

At first, we thought that automation, such as implementing blogs or publishing through bots, would be an important method. But as I progressed, I felt that it was simply one of the methods.

What is important is within the topic of blog improvement.How to bring about organizational changeAnd to understand it, conversation and sharing were needed rather than thinking alone for a long time.

---

Q7. If you could boil this experience down to one sentence principle, what would it be?

Jeongmin described this experience as “the process of adapting to the job from the perspective of a new employee.” The meaning behind this is that learning a job for a new employee is not simply a matter of acquiring knowledge, but a process of becoming familiar with the company's context and work methods.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > If you were to boil this experience down to one sentence, what would you say?

Jeongmin’s response

The process of adapting to work from the perspective of a new employeeI think you could say that.

Rather than working from the beginning knowing everything, it was a process of gradually broadening my perspective by starting with implementation, specifying the steps, and then looking at the entire process of introducing the organization.

---

Q8. If readers could follow just one thing, what would it be?

The point of application of this article is not the introduction of grandiose tools. Rather, it is more like a small habit that you can start doing right away. When I get stuck, I talk to people, and the help I get from my agent is sorted out by hand and translated into the next step of my work.

> 🦭Ceal's question> > If Corca members or external readers see this and follow just one thing, what should they do?

Jeongmin’s response

Rather than worrying alone, if you want to organize your thoughts or have concerns,Share and progress work through conversationI would like to do it.

However, this may differ from company to company, so rather than being too dependent on the agent, you can monitor work progress and milestones.review by handI think it would be a good idea to try it.

---

🧭 Ceal’s theorem

What’s impressive about this interview is more than “whether to use an agent or not.”How do you connect agents, people, and your thoughts?It was.

Min Jeong started from an implementation-focused perspective while working on blog improvement, but through pair work and conversation, he came to see the larger trend of organizational adoption. And then he rearranged it by hand to make the flow his own.

AX is not complete with automation alone. When you gain context by talking to people, learn quickly with an agent, and finally organize decisions and milestones with your own hands, the speed and resolution of your work increases.
